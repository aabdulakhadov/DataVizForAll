# Government Debt Performance During and After the Global Financial Crisis
The slope chart below illustrates the government debt performance (as a percentage of GDP) of selected countries during two critical periods:
- Crisis and Immediate Aftermath (2008–2013): This period captures the impact of the global financial crisis and the subsequent recovery years. During this time, many countries saw significant increases in government debt as a percentage of GDP due to economic contraction, stimulus spending, and measures taken to stabilize financial systems.

- Post-Crisis Recovery and Recent Trends (2013–2018): This phase represents the global recovery and recent trends following the crisis. Some countries managed to stabilize or reduce their debt levels as a percentage of GDP through economic recovery and fiscal consolidation. However, for others, debt levels remained high or continued to increase due to persistent economic challenges or policy decisions.

The slope chart provides a visual comparison of changes in government debt over these two periods, highlighting how different countries navigated the crisis, the pace of recovery, and recent trends. The steeper the slope, the more significant the change in debt levels between the two periods.
